NLL/MAE compare only *within* a likelihood; perplexity, utilization, and separability compare across all configs.

## Interpretation

- **Reconstruction (NB family).** Among the NB configs, `nb-2x256` reaches the lowest held-out NLL (218.3) at MAE 1.222. NLL/MAE are only comparable for a *fixed* likelihood — the Gaussian head targets `log1p` expression, so its NLL/MAE are not on the NB scale and are omitted from this ranking.
- **Codebook utilization.** Highest utilization is `nb-2x16` (0.8438 of entries used, perplexity 9.031). `nb-2x256`, `nb-1x64` fall below 50% utilization — a sign the larger codebooks are under-used at this data scale (expected: more entries than the data needs).
- **Downstream separability.** `nb-2x16` best preserves the known programs in its latent (nearest-centroid accuracy 1). Separability uses the continuous pre-quantization latent, so it *is* comparable across likelihoods and codebook sizes.
- **Raw-count NB vs log-normalized Gaussian (2 x 64).** Separability is 0.9958 (NB) vs 0.4875 (Gaussian); the raw-count NB model keeps the programs better separated here. NB/ZINB is the right default for count data, and the gap is expected to persist or widen on real, overdispersed counts.
- **Batch effects.** The fixture carries a synthetic `batch` covariate but no batch-driven signal; the unconditional v1 model separates the programs without conditioning, consistent with deferring batch conditioning until a real-data benchmark shows it is needed.

## Caveats

- The fixture is a clean synthetic Poisson signal; absolute numbers are illustrative. The harness contract (`omvqvae.benchmark.run_suite`) is identical for a local-AnnData `DataLoader` or a Census stream, so this report regenerates against real data by swapping the fixture's training source.
- Separability is a resubstitution nearest-centroid proxy (an optimistic upper bound) intended for *relative* comparison, not absolute classifier accuracy.
